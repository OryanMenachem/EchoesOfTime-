Part 3

What Was Left Behind

Even though Louis Braille invented the system when he was still young, many people didn’t accept it at first. Some teachers and school leaders thought it was too different. They didn’t want to change their old ways.

For many years, the Braille system was not taught in schools. Louis died in 1852, and during his life, he didn’t see the full success of his invention.

But he left behind something powerful—a complete system for reading and writing that blind people could use. His tools, his notes, and the support of his fellow students were all part of what he left.
