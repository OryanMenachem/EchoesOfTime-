Part 2

Who Was Involved

Louis Braille was only a boy when he began working on a better way for blind people to read and write. At the school in Paris, he met other blind students who also had trouble with the reading system.

He also met teachers who supported him, like Dr. Pignier, the school director, who believed in him. Louis studied a method used by the French army to send silent messages at night using raised dots.

He improved that method and, at age 15, created a new system using just six dots in different positions. His friends at school helped him test it. Together, they made it work.
